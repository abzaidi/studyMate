
Q: What are the two main components of any kind of computer?
A: Any type of computer consists of two primary components: hardware and software. Hardware refers to the physical parts that make up a computer system.

Q: What is hardware in a computer?
A: Hardware is the collection of physical parts that make up a computer system, including components such as monitors, keyboards, data storage devices, graphic cards, sound cards, motherboards, and chips.

Q: What is the function of input devices?
A: Input devices are used to translate data into a format that can be understood by a computer, enabling it to process information from humans. Examples of input devices include keyboards, mice, microphones, and touchscreens.

Q: What is the Central Processing Unit (CPU)?
A: The Central Processing Unit (CPU) serves as the brain of a computer and is primarily responsible for processing information. It takes instructions from software and executes them, enabling the computer to perform various tasks efficiently.


Q: What are the types of operations executed by the Arithmetic Logical Unit (ALU)?
A: The Arithmetic Logical Unit (ALU) executes various types of operations, primarily focused on arithmetic and logical processes. It performs basic arithmetic calculations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. These operations enable the processing unit to perform mathematical tasks and logical decisions in digital circuits and computers.
